Executive summary

A three-location service business runs strong clinical or guest-facing quality, but digital intake and handoffs leak revenue. First human reply on high-intent channels averages 4–7 hours during business days; follow-up stops after one attempt for roughly 38% of open inquiries.

We mapped five journey stages, flagged three gaps with evidence from CRM timestamps and channel exports, and scoped one 14-day pilot that does not require a new platform on day one.

Operational gaps (excerpt)

Gap 1 — Inquiry routing

Web forms and WhatsApp land in separate inboxes. No SLA, no owner after first touch. Median time-to-first-reply: 4h 12m (business hours).

Gap 2 — Stale pipeline

47 leads sat in “contacted” with no activity for 14+ days. Coordinators lacked a single re-engagement playbook.

Gap 3 — Handoff to booking

Treatment or stay coordinators re-keyed details already captured at intake. Estimated 12–18 min lost per converted inquiry.

Prioritized fixes

  1. Intake SLA + owner — one queue, 60-minute first-response target on WhatsApp and web.
  2. Stale-stage sweep — weekly batch with templated second touch and close rules.
  3. Booking handoff card — required fields passed automatically from first qualified reply.

Recommended pilot

14-day intake & follow-up sprint. Implement the SLA queue and stale re-engagement for one location first. Success = median first reply under 90 minutes and 25% fewer 14-day stale leads—measured from existing CRM exports, not new dashboards.
